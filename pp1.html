Track Description

This piece is a cover/rendition of "Give Yourself A Try" by The 1975. I recreated every instrumental part of the original song as heard in the chorus, but with a couple added instruments at the beginning to ease into the chorus. The Vocal track is the direct recording of the original band's Vocals.

The tempo of the song is 183 BPM. The song is composed of an overly-compressed electronic Drum Kit, a Bass Guitar, a couple of Synths to fill the EQ space, an obnoxious Lead Guitar, a chiller Backing Guitar, and of course, the Vocals.

The drums were mainly samples from Logic's Atlanta Drum Kit, with a few replacement samples for the Snare Drum and Crash Symbol. The Bass guitar was a standard Stinger Bass ran through quite a signal chain of amps and compressors. The multiple stages of compression on both the drums and bass were done to most accurately reflect how the original track sounded.

The key of the song is B Major. The Harmony Track is called "Chords", and Its Chord Progression is: | B, Eb, E | B, Eb #9, Emaj7 | B, Eb, E |.

The melody MIDI track is the "Synth" track, where it uses a simple octave interval melody repeating every measure of the whole song. (B2, B3, B3.) This provides the basis but reverse-note melody for the Audio tracks which carry the main guitar and vocal melodies.

The synth track was made using an ES-P Synthesizer based around square waves and slow attacks/releases.

In terms of the sampler based track, The Chords track was an EX24 Sampler Synth with a soft attack/release and a little glide at the start of each chord to give the track an easy introduction.

The two Guitar Audio Tracks are all recordings of me playing my Fender Strat guitar. They're the same interval Octave based melody, quantized so they're both perfectly in time with each other/the song, but one is chiller and one is super distorted and loud.

And lastly, the Vocal track is the direct recording of the original vocals from the original song it self. I wish I could sing to do them myself, but I can't.

In terms of Dynamic processing, I added a second VCA Compressor to the bass to really fatten up and saturate the sound. I also tuned the Drum Buss compressor to my own custom over-driven setting in order to re-create the effect heard in the original song.

For time-based processing, I have a Reverb Buss that uses Logic's Space Designer, and I loaded one of the vocal plates for it. I run the guitars and synths to here a little bit. I chose this plate to add more umph to the mix because of its shortness but also spacey feel. I also have a Delay Buss with the Delay Designer on a 1/16th delay to make the backing guitar a little more rhythmic/present in the mix.
